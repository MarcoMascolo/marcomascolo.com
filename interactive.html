Play around with the slicing to feel what I am talking about: take a shape, cut it with a flat one dimension lower, and watch what the smaller world actually sees. cross-sections · the slicing principle

Slicing a shape one dimension down

An N-dimensional object cut by an (N−1)-dimensional flat gives a lower-dimensional cross-section. A round object only changes size. A faceted object changes its number of sides. Step up the dimensions and drive the slice yourself.
