cross-sections · the slicing principle

Slicing a shape one dimension down

An N-dimensional object cut by an (N−1)-dimensional flat gives a lower-dimensional cross-section. A round object only changes size. A faceted object changes its number of sides. Step up the dimensions and drive the slice yourself.

1D · point slices a segment
2D · line slices a shape
3D · plane slices a solid
4D · space slices a tesseract
a block of spacetime · velocity · time · gravity

Two people, two nows, in one static block

An illustration, not a literal simulation. The whole box is spacetime: floor is space, up is time, the pale walls are the light cone. Each person has a coloured slice, their now. Speed them up toward light speed, sweep time, and drop a mass in to bend the slices.
